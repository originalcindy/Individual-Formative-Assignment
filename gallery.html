Age Equality

Age equality is the idea that all people should be treated equally, regardless of their age, and have access to the same rights and opportunities. The Equality Act 2010 in the UK protects people from age discrimination in a number of ways.

Ways in which age inequality occur

  • Direct discrimination

Happens When someone is treated worse than another person in a similar situation because of their age. When an organization has a policy or way of working that puts people of a certain age group at a disadvantage

  • Harassment

When someone makes you feel humiliated, offended, or degraded.

  • Victimisation

When you are treated badly because you have made a complaint of age discrimination. The Equality Act also requires public bodies to consider the needs of older people when planning or carrying out their public duties or services.

    Who it affects and why

age discrimination can affect people of all ages, but it particularly affects young and older people. The COVID-19 pandemic has highlighted the intersectionality of age with other protected characteristics such as ethnicity and socio-economic status.

Age inequality can occur in several key areas

  • Employment

    • Older applicants may be overlooked in favor of younger candidates perceived as more tech-savvy, while younger candidates may be deemed inexperienced. Age can influence who gets promoted, with older workers sometimes being passed over for younger, less experienced individuals.

  • Wages

    • Pay disparities can arise, where younger employees are paid less due to inexperience, while older workers may face wage stagnation or be offered lower salaries due to biases about their age.

  • Training and Development

    • Older workers may receive fewer opportunities for skill development, while younger workers might not be given the chance to prove their capabilities due to stereotypes about their age.

  • Healthcare Access

    • Older adults might face age-related biases in medical care, leading to inadequate treatment, while younger individuals may struggle to access certain services that cater to their needs.

  • Social Services

    • Allocation of resources, such as funding for youth programs or elderly care, can disproportionately benefit one age group over another.

  • Cultural Representation

    • Media often perpetuates stereotypes about different age groups, leading to a lack of diverse and accurate representations of both younger and older people.

  • Housing

    • Discrimination in housing can occur, with older adults facing challenges in finding suitable accommodations, while younger individuals may struggle with affordability.
